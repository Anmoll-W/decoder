# Session Template

At the end of any full ASYNC session, offer this to the user. They can save it to their vault to build a personal knowledge base over time.

Tell them:
> *"Here's a note you can save if you want to come back to this:"*

Then output the filled-in template below.

---

**Suggested vault path:** `Knowledge/decoder/[topic-slug]/[YYYY-MM-DD].md`

---

```markdown
# [Topic] — [YYYY-MM-DD]

**Mode:** ASYNC
**Level:** Junior / Mid / Senior  
**Depth:** 2-minute / Full breakdown

---

## The one-line version
[Bottom-line explanation in one sentence — what it is and why it matters]

## The analogy that made it click
[The character analogy used in this session, written out fully]

## The thing most people miss
[The earned reveal or non-obvious insight from the session]

## Smart question to ask my engineer
[The close question from Phase 6, specific to this session]

---

## Quiz result
- [ ] Passed on first attempt
- [ ] Passed after reteach (gap was: [X])
- [ ] Skipped quiz

---

## What to look up next
[1-2 adjacent concepts mentioned during the session worth exploring]
```

---

## Notes on when to offer the template

- **Offer:** After any ASYNC full breakdown session
- **Offer:** After any ASYNC 2-min session where the user asked follow-up questions
- **Skip:** LIVE sessions — too short, the close question is the takeaway
- **Skip:** DECISION sessions — the stance and pressure-test question are the takeaway

The template is a tool for the user's vault, not a mandatory output. Always frame it as optional.
